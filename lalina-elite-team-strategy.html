05
Risk Management
Critical Risks + Expert Mitigations
Meta Account Ban / iOS Attribution Loss
Severity: High Likelihood: High
Single-channel dependency on Meta + declining pixel accuracy post-iOS 14 means your attribution data may be 25–40% inaccurate today. If Meta bans the account, revenue could drop 70% overnight.
Deploy server-side Meta CAPI immediately (Claude Code task). Cross-reference Meta ROAS with GA4 + Klaviyo. Build email list to 5K before scaling Meta above $150/day. Google + Pinterest must reach 30% of traffic by Month 4.
Stockout During Ad Scaling
Severity: High Likelihood: Medium
Scaling Meta to $200+/day while a hero SKU is at 12 units is one of the most expensive DTC mistakes. You pay for clicks you cannot convert. Lost ROAS, wasted spend, and damaged pixel learning.
Set WooCommerce stock alerts at 20 units (Claude Code lalina-stock-system.php). Pause all ads for that SKU when stock hits 10. Maintain 8-week rolling buffer on SLOANE and top 3 SKUs. Plan restock before every ad scaling event.
Founder Burnout — Single Point of Failure
Severity: High Likelihood: High
The playbook has 47+ tasks across ads, content, tech, email, fulfilment, and strategy. One person managing all of this simultaneously hits a wall around $15–25K/month. Quality degrades across all channels.
Document every process as an SOP from Week 1. Hire a part-time VA by Month 2 (social scheduling + customer support = 15hrs/week freed). Hire fractional email specialist by Month 5. Founder moves to strategy-only by Month 6.
Photography Bottleneck
Severity: High Likelihood: Medium
Without professional lifestyle photography, paid ads underperform by 30–50% compared to brands with a proper creative library. This is the single biggest creative constraint on ROAS above $50K/month.
Commission a brand shoot by Month 3 ($800–2,000 budget). Deliverables: 10 lifestyle shots per hero bag + 5 product-only + 10 detail shots. This creative investment typically improves ROAS by 20–40% within 2 weeks of deployment.
Reactive Discounting Trap
Severity: Medium Likelihood: High
When sales slow, the instinct is a sitewide discount. This already exists as a permanent 20% off and is actively destroying the brand's perceived value and AOV. Customers learn to wait for sales.
Remove blanket discount immediately (48-hour action). Replace with: flash sales on 2–3 SKUs for 72 hours only. When sales slow: improve the creative, fix the landing page, or launch a new product angle — never discount reactively.
Rising CPMs + Competitor Bidding
Severity: Medium Likelihood: High
Meta CPMs seasonally rise 40–60% (Q4, Valentine's, Mother's Day). Competitors in leather handbags will bid on your brand name in Google. Higher CPMs compress ROAS without warning.
Launch Brand Search campaign on Google immediately ($5–10/day) to protect your brand name. Pre-plan creative refresh before Q4 and Mother's Day. When CPMs spike, shift budget to Google, email reactivation, and Pinterest — which doesn't auction the same way.

Expert Gap Analysis
What the Playbook Doesn't Fully Address
Site Speed & Core Web Vitals
The playbook fixes image lazy-loading but doesn't audit overall page speed. A WooCommerce site with unoptimized images, no CDN, and multiple conflicting plugins can load in 5–8 seconds on mobile — a 40% bounce rate driver. Google Shopping quality score also depends on landing page speed.
Run a Google PageSpeed Insights test on lalinabags.com immediately. Target LCP under 2.5s. Install a CDN (Cloudflare free tier), compress all product images to WebP, and audit for plugin conflicts causing render-blocking JS.
Checkout Abandonment Rate
The playbook addresses cart abandonment (pre-checkout) but not checkout abandonment (post-cart). Industry average checkout abandonment is 70%. If the checkout page requires account creation, has too many fields, or lacks trust signals, revenue is leaking at the final step.
Enable WooCommerce guest checkout. Add trust badges inside checkout: padlock icon, payment logos, "256-bit secure checkout." Reduce form fields to minimum. Add order summary with product image on checkout page. Test one-page checkout if your theme supports it.
Tax & Compliance for International Sales
The playbook targets UK, Germany, UAE, and 7 other currency markets. Selling into the EU requires VAT compliance (especially post-Brexit UK and EU OSS rules). Failure to collect VAT correctly can result in significant legal liability at scale.
Install a tax compliance plugin (TaxJar or Avalara for WooCommerce) before international campaigns scale. Understand UK VAT threshold (£85K). Consider registering for EU VAT OSS scheme. This is not optional at the $100K+ revenue stage.
Customer Lifetime Value Measurement
The playbook sets LTV targets but doesn't specify exactly how to measure or track it in WooCommerce. Without accurate LTV data, you cannot know your true customer acquisition cost ceiling — meaning you may be under-spending on ads when you could safely scale further.
Build a cohort LTV report in GA4 or use Klaviyo's predicted LTV feature. Track: first purchase date, second purchase rate at 90 days, average orders per customer per year. This number determines how aggressively you can bid on cold traffic CPAs.
Returns Processing & Rate Tracking
The playbook mentions -20% returns as a benefit of the size guide but doesn't set up a returns tracking system. High return rates (above 12% for leather bags) can wipe margin gains from ROAS improvements. The cause of each return needs to be identified and fixed.
Tag every return with a reason code in WooCommerce. Review weekly. If "not as expected" exceeds 30% of returns, your product photography or descriptions are the problem. If "quality issue" appears consistently, escalate to supplier QA review immediately.
Wholesale & B2B Channel Preparation
The playbook mentions wholesale at Month 6–7 but provides no preparation. Boutique buyers expect a trade price list, minimum order quantities, lookbook PDF, and payment terms before any meeting. Arriving without these documents loses the opportunity.
Create a wholesale one-pager by Month 4: brand story, trade price list (typically 50% of RRP), MOQ policy, lead times, and 5–10 hero product images. A clean two-page PDF built in the Lalina brand system opens doors that a cold email cannot.

Expert Recommendations
Non-Negotiable Best Practices
Technical Discipline
  • Always Use UTM parameters on every paid link. Without this, GA4 misattributes revenue and you cannot trust channel-level data.
  • Never Trust Meta reported ROAS as your single source of truth. Cross-reference with GA4 + Klaviyo. Meta overcounts; GA4 undercounts. The truth is between them.
  • Always Test every Claude Code deployment in incognito + mobile before going live. One untested PHP snippet can break checkout.
  • Never Install more than one caching or optimization plugin. They conflict. Choose one: WP Rocket, W3 Total Cache, or LiteSpeed. One only.
  • Always Keep a child theme. All PHP customizations go in the child theme functions.php — never in the parent theme. Updates wipe parent theme changes.
  • Never Run Performance Max campaigns until you have 200+ tracked purchases. PMax without conversion data burns budget on irrelevant placements.
Brand & Commercial Discipline
  • Always Frame offers as events, not permanent states. A 72-hour sale on 3 SKUs builds urgency. A permanent sitewide discount destroys margin and trains customers to wait.
  • Never Scale ad spend without creative refresh. After 7–14 days, creative fatigue sets in. Weekly creative testing is not optional at $200+/day.
  • Always Respond to every review within 24 hours in brand voice. Public responses to reviews are read by 10× more people than wrote the review.
  • Never Buy followers, reviews, or engagement. Lalina's verified photo reviews are a genuine competitive moat. Protect them absolutely.
  • Always Document every process before delegating it. An undocumented process cannot be handed off without quality loss.
  • Never Send a broadcast email to your full list more than twice per week. Email fatigue is real. Every unnecessary send erodes the list's revenue-generating power.
