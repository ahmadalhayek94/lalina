// 1. SCARCITY DISPLAY on product page
function lalina_stock_urgency() {
  global $product;
  $stock = $product->get_stock_quantity();

  if ($stock === null) return;

  if ($stock <= 5 && $stock > 0) {
    echo '<p class="lalina-scarcity">
      <span class="dot">●</span> Only ' . $stock . ' left in this colour
    </p>';
  }
}
add_action('woocommerce_single_product_summary', 'lalina_stock_urgency', 25);

// 2. ADMIN EMAIL ALERT on purchase triggers low stock
function lalina_low_stock_alert($order_id) {
  $order = wc_get_order($order_id);
  foreach ($order->get_items() as $item) {
    $product = $item->get_product();
    $stock   = $product->get_stock_quantity();
    if ($stock !== null && $stock <= 5) {
      wp_mail(
        get_option('admin_email'),
        '⚠️ Lalina Low Stock: ' . $product->get_name(),
        'SKU: '.$product->get_sku().' | Stock remaining: '.$stock
      );
    }
  }
}
add_action('woocommerce_order_status_processing', 'lalina_low_stock_alert');

Step 08
Daily / Weekly Execution System
Operational Rhythm — Three Cadences
Daily Tasks
Every working day — 60–90 min total
  • Check Meta Ads Manager: CTR, ROAS, spend vs. budget. Pause any ad set with ROAS < 1.5 after $50 spend.
  • Review Klaviyo dashboard: open rates, click rates, revenue from flows vs. campaigns.
  • Process all new customer reviews: respond to every review within 24 hours (brand voice, warm).
  • Check WooCommerce orders: flag any fulfilment issues, track returns or complaints.
  • Post 1 Instagram Story: product feature, customer repost, or behind-scenes brand moment.
  • Monitor stock levels: reorder any SKU below 20 units to avoid stockouts during ad scaling.
  • Check GA4: session count, bounce rate, top exit pages — identify any sudden UX issues.
  • Reply to all DMs and comments within 4 hours during business hours (brand voice).
Weekly Tasks
Monday or Friday — 3–4 hours
  • Meta Ads weekly review: identify top-performing creatives, pause bottom 20%, plan next week's tests.
  • Email campaign send: 1 broadcast email per week to full list (new arrivals, editorial, or sale event).
  • Publish 1–2 SEO blog articles (Claude-assisted). Upload to WordPress with proper meta descriptions.
  • Schedule all social content for the coming week (Planoly or Buffer): 7 posts + 10 Stories minimum.
  • Influencer check-in: review this week's gifted creator content. Repost UGC to brand IG. Request usage rights.
  • Review Klaviyo flow performance: identify underperforming emails. A/B test subject lines.
  • Check Google Ads: Search Terms report — add irrelevant search terms as negatives weekly.
  • Weekly revenue report: total revenue, AOV, units sold, top SKUs, conversion rate vs. prior week.
Monthly Tasks
First Monday of month — Half day
  • Full P&L review: revenue vs. ad spend vs. COGS vs. net margin. Adjust budgets for following month.
  • CRO audit: review conversion rate by page. Identify the one page with the biggest drop-off and fix it.
  • Email list hygiene: suppress unengaged subscribers (no open in 90 days) to protect deliverability.
  • Influencer programme audit: review which creators drove actual sales (UTM tracking). Cut non-performers.
  • Inventory review: stock count vs. sales velocity. Place restock orders with 6-week lead time buffer.
  • New A/B test launch: set up one new CRO test on the highest-traffic product page.
  • SEO ranking check: track keyword positions for top 20 target keywords using Google Search Console.
  • Competitive audit: review 3 competitor brands — pricing, new products, campaigns, positioning changes.
  • Content planning session: plan next month's editorial calendar, campaign themes, and photo needs.
  • Strategic review: compare current month vs. milestone targets. Adjust tactics if off-track.

Critical Operational Bottlenecks
Bottleneck 1 — Photography
Current State → RiskWithout professional brand photography, paid ads underperform and social content looks amateurish. This is the single biggest creative limiter on growth past $50K/month.

Fix: Commission a brand shoot within 30 days. Budget: $800–2,000. Deliver: 10 lifestyle shots + 5 product-only + 10 detail shots per hero bag. Use for 6+ months across all channels.
Bottleneck 2 — Founder Time
Current State → RiskIf one person manages ads, content, fulfilment, customer support, and strategy simultaneously, all of them suffer. Growth stalls around $15–25K/month without delegation.

Fix: Hire a part-time VA ($400–600/month) for social scheduling and customer support replies by Month 2. This frees 15+ hours/week for revenue-generating activity.
Bottleneck 3 — Inventory Capital
Current State → RiskScaling ads requires having inventory ready to ship. Stockouts during a successful campaign are one of the most expensive mistakes in e-commerce — you pay for clicks you can't convert.

Fix: Build a rolling 8-week inventory buffer. Consider a small business line of credit (or Shopify Capital equivalent for WooCommerce) to fund inventory ahead of ad scaling phases.
Marketing Risk Safeguards
  • Never run on one channel:If Meta is 90%+ of your traffic, you are one algorithm update from zero. Build Google, Pinterest, and email to 40% combined before scaling Meta above $200/day.
  • Never run without server-side tracking:Claude Code task: "Set up WooCommerce server-side Meta Conversions API (CAPI) integration. Output: complete functions.php code using Meta Business SDK for PHP." Without CAPI, you're flying blind on attribution.
  • Never buy fake reviews or fake followers:One credible exposé kills years of brand trust. Lalina's verified photo reviews are a genuine competitive asset. Protect them by keeping them authentic.
  • Never discount reactively:If sales slow, the instinct is to offer a sitewide discount. Resist. Instead: improve the ad creative, fix the landing page, or launch a new product angle. Discounts train customers to wait for sales.

The Non-Negotiable Rules for Getting to $1M
  • Fix before you scale:Every dollar spent on ads before fixing the image loading issue, the product descriptions, and the trust elements is 40% wasted. The foundation must be solid before the house is built.
  • Email is the profit engine:Build the list from Day 1. At $1M/year, email should generate $250,000–$300,000 of that — with zero ad spend. Every subscriber is an asset you own forever.
  • Creative is the competitive advantage:In paid ads, the creative IS the targeting. Better photography and better copy beat better audience targeting every single time. Invest in the creative before increasing spend.
  • Never stop reviewing the data:The market will tell you what's working if you listen. The best DTC operators are obsessive about their numbers — not because they love data, but because data tells you where to put the next dollar.
  • Brand is the moat:At $1M/year, the brand story, the packaging, the customer reviews, the social following — these are harder to replicate than the product itself. Build them deliberately and protect them aggressively.
